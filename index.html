Egg hopper

Hoppers, also known as appa, are bowl-shaped pancakes made from fermented rice flour and coconut milk. The addition of an egg to the center of the hopper creates an egg hopper. The batter is poured into a special hopper pan, and as it cooks, an egg is cracked into the center, giving it a unique and delicious flavor.

rice and curry

Rice and curry is the quintessential and staple dish of Sri Lankan cuisine. The meal typically consists of rice accompanied by a selection of curries, which can include meat, fish, poultry, or vegetarian options. The curries are often flavored with a variety of spices, such as cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and curry leaves, creating a complex and satisfying taste.

Rice is a good source of carbohydrates, while the curries offer proteins, vitamins, and minerals. The inclusion of vegetables, coconut, and various spices adds nutritional value to the overall meal.

Authentic drinks

Kola Kanda

This is a traditional Sri Lankan porridge that holds both cultural significance and nutritional value. Made from a variety of green leafy vegetables, rice, and aromatic spices, this hearty dish is often enjoyed as a nutritious breakfast

Nelli Crush (Gooseberry Juice)

Made from the tart gooseberry fruit, Nelli Crush is a tangy beverage often sweetened with jaggery or sugar, providing a delightful contrast of flavors..

Thambili

Also known as king coconut water, taken from young green coconuts, this drink is delicious and hydrating at the same time.
